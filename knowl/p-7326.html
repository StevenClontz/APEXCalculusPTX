The SI unit of force is the newton; one newton is equal to one kg·m⁄s2, and the SI unit of distance is a meter (m). The fundamental unit of work is one newton–meter, or a joule (J). That is, applying a force of one newton for one meter performs one joule of work. In Imperial units (as used in the United States), force is measured in pounds (lb) and distance is measured in feet (ft), hence work is measured in ft–lb.
