Circle Revisited.

We implicitly found the derivative of \(x^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) the unit circle, in Section 2.6 Example 2.6.11 as \(\lz{y}{x} = -x/y\text{.}\) In Example 3.1.28, half of the unit circle is given as \(y=f(x) = \sqrt{1-x^2}\text{.}\)

We found \(\fp(x) = -x\big/\sqrt{1-x^2}\text{.}\) Recognize that the denominator of this fraction is \(y\text{;}\) that is, we again found \(\fp(x) = \lz{y}{x} = -x/y\text{.}\)
