Theorem 11.4.6. Properties of the Cross Product.

Let \(\vecu\text{,}\) \(\vecv\) and \(\vecw\) be vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) and let \(c\) be a scalar. The following identities hold:

  1. \(\crossp uv = -(\crossp vu)\) Anticommutative Property

    1. \((\vec u+\vec v)\times \vecw = \crossp uw+\crossp vw\) Distributive Properties

    2. \(\displaystyle \vec u \times (\vec v+\vec w) = \crossp uv+\crossp uw\)

  3. \(\displaystyle c(\crossp uv) = (c\vecu) \times \vec v = \vecu \times (c\vecv)\)

    1. \((\crossp uv)\cdot \vecu = 0\) Orthogonality Properties

    2. \(\displaystyle (\crossp uv)\cdot \vecv = 0\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \crossp uu = \vec 0\)

  6. \(\displaystyle \crossp u0 = \vec 0\)

  7. \(\vecu \cdot (\vecv\times\vecw) = (\crossp uv)\cdot \vecw\) Triple Scalar Product
