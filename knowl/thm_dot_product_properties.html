Theorem 11.3.4. Properties of the Dot Product.

Let \(\vec u\text{,}\) \(\vec v\) and \(\vec w\) be vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) or \(\mathbb{R}^3\) and let \(c\) be a scalar.

  1. \(\dotp uv = \dotp vu\) {Commutative Property}

  2. \(\vec u\cdot(\vec v+\vec w) = \dotp uv + \dotp uw\) {Distributive Property}

  3. \(\displaystyle c(\dotp uv) = (c\vec u)\cdot \vec v = \vec u \cdot (c\vec v)\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \dotp 0v = 0\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \dotp vv=\norm{\vec v}^2\)
