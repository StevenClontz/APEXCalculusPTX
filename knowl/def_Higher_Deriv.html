Definition 2.3.12. Higher Order Derivatives.

Let \(y=f(x)\) be a differentiable function on \(I\text{.}\) The following are defined, provided the corresponding limits exist.

  1. The second derivative of \(f\) is:

    \begin{equation*} \fp'(x) = \lzoo{x}{\fp(x)} = \lzoo{x}{\lz{y}{x}} = \lzn{2}{y}{x}=y''\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. The third derivative of \(f\) is:

    \begin{equation*} \fp''(x) = \lzoo{x}{\fp'(x)} = \lzoo{x}{\lzn{2}{y}{x}} = \lzn{3}{y}{x}=y'''\text{.} \end{equation*}

  3. The \(n\)th derivative of \(f\) is:

    \begin{equation*} f^{(n)}(x) = \lzoo{x}{f^{(n-1)}(x)} = \lzoo{x}{\lzn{n-1}{y}{x}} = \lzn{n}{y}{x}=y^{(n)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
