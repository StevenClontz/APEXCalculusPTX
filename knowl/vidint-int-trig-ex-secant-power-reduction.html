Integrals involving odd powers of \(\sec(x)\) (and nothing else) are often among the more intimidating tasks for beginning calculus students. However, larger odd powers are best handled not by doing the integral directly, but by employing a reduction forumula. The video below shows how to obtain a reduction formula for the integral of \(\sec^{2k+1}(x)\text{;}\) this formula allows us to express the integral in terms of an integral where the power of \(\sec(x)\) is reduced by two.
