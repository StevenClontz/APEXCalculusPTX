Solution 15.3.15.1.

A parametrization is already given for \(C\text{,}\) so we just need to find \(\vec F\big(\vec r(t)\big)\) and \vec r '(t).

We have \(\vec F\big(\vec r(t)\big) = \la -\sin t, \cos t, 1\ra\) and \(\vrp(t) = \la -\sin t, \cos t, 1/(2\pi)\ra\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{align*} \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r \amp = \int_0^{2\pi} \la -\sin t, \cos t, 1\ra\cdot \la -\sin t, \cos t, 1/(2\pi)\ra dt\\ \amp = \int_0^{2\pi} \Big(\sin^2t+\cos^2t + \frac1{2\pi}\big)dt\\ \amp = 2\pi + 1 \approx 7.28\text{.} \end{align*}
