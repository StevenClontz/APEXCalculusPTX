We begin by representing a planar lamina with a region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane with density function \(\delta(x,y)\text{.}\) Partition \(R\) into \(n\) subdivisions, each with area \(\Delta A_i\text{.}\) As done before, we can approximate the mass of the \(i\)th subregion with \(\delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i\text{,}\) where \((x_i,y_i)\) is a point inside the \(i\)th subregion. We can approximate the moment of this subregion about the \(y\)-axis with \(x_i\delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i\) â€” that is, by multiplying the approximate mass of the region by its approximate distance from the \(y\)-axis. Similarly, we can approximate the moment about the \(x\)-axis with \(y_i\delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i\text{.}\) By summing over all subregions, we have:

\begin{align*} \text{ mass: } M \amp \approx \sum_{i=1}^n \delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i \text{ (as seen before) }\\ \text{ moment about the \(x\)-axis: } M_x \amp \approx \sum_{i=1}^n y_i\delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i\\ \text{ moment about the \(y\)-axis: } M_y \amp \approx \sum_{i=1}^n x_i\delta(x_i,y_i)\Delta A_i \end{align*}
