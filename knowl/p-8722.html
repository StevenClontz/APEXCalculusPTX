Since the Integral Test applies, we know that \(a(x)\) must be a decreasing function. The sum \(R_n = a_{n+1}+a_{n+2}+\cdots\) can be viewed as a right-endpoint Riemann sum for the integral \(\int_n^\infty a(x)\,dx\text{,}\) and this must be an underestimate, since \(a(x)\) is a decreasing function. But \(R_n\) can also be viewed as a left-endpoint Riemann sum for the integral \(\int_{n+1}^\infty a(x)\,dx\text{,}\) and this must be an overestimate.
