In the following exercises, a region of the Cartesian plane is described. Use the Shell Method to find the volume of the solid of revolution formed by rotating the region about each of the given axes.

13.

Region bounded by: \(y=\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(y=0\) and \(x=1\text{.}\)

Rotate about:

  1. the \(y\)-axis

  2. \(\displaystyle x=1\)

  3. the \(x\)-axis

  4. \(\displaystyle y=1\)

14.

Region bounded by: \(y=4-x^2\) and \(y=0\text{.}\)

Rotate about:

  1. \(\displaystyle x=2\)

  2. \(\displaystyle x=-2\)

  3. the \(x\)-axis

  4. \(\displaystyle y=4\)

15.

The triangle with vertices \((1,1)\text{,}\) \((1,2)\) and \((2,1)\text{.}\)

Rotate about:

  1. the \(y\)-axis

  2. \(\displaystyle x=1\)

  3. the \(x\)-axis

  4. \(\displaystyle y=2\)

16.

Region bounded by \(y=x^2-2x+2\) and \(y=2x-1\text{.}\)

Rotate about:

  1. the \(y\)-axis

  2. \(\displaystyle x=1\)

  3. \(\displaystyle x=-1\)

17.

Region bounded by \(y=1/\sqrt{x^2+1}\text{,}\) \(x=1\) and the \(x\) and \(y\)-axes.

Rotate about:

  1. the \(y\)-axis

  2. \(\displaystyle x=1\)

18.

Region bounded by \(y=2x\text{,}\) \(y=x\) and \(x=2\text{.}\)

Rotate about:

  1. the \(y\)-axis

  2. \(\displaystyle x=2\)

  3. the \(x\)-axis

  4. \(\displaystyle y=4\)
