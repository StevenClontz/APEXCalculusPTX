The domain of \(f\) is \(\mathbb{R}\) (you can take the odd root of both positive and negative nubmers). Next, we take the first derivative. Since we know we want to solve \(\fp(x) = 0\text{,}\) we will do some algebra after taking the derivative.

\begin{align*} f(x) \amp = x^{\frac{8}{3}}-4x^{\frac{2}{3}}\\ \fp(x) \amp = \frac{8}{3} x^{\frac{5}{3}} - \frac{8}{3}x^{-\frac{1}{3}}\\ \amp = \frac{8}{3}x^{-\frac{1}{3}}\left(x^{\frac{6}{3}}-1\right)\\ \amp =\frac{8}{3}x^{-\frac{1}{3}}\left(x^2-1\right)\\ \amp =\frac{8}{3}x^{-\frac{1}{3}}(x-1)(x+1)\text{.} \end{align*}
