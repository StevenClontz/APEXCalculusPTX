Theorem 5.4.7. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part 1.

Let \(f\) be continuous on \([a,b]\) and let \(F(x) = \int_a^x f(t)\, dt\text{.}\) Then \(F\) is a differentiable function on \((a,b)\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} \Fp(x)=f(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

In other words:

\begin{equation*} \lzoo{x}{\int_a^x f(t)\, dt}=f(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}
