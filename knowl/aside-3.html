Many people like to remember the Sum Property as stating that “the limit of the sum is the sum of the limits”, and the Product Property as stating that the “limit of a product is the product of the limits.”

In practice, the Scalar Multiple Property is often viewed as telling us that we can “take constants out of limits”:

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to c}(b\cdot f(x)) = b\cdot\lim_{x\to c}f(x). \end{equation*}
