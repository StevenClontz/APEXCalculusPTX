Solution 13.8.3.13.1.

One critical point: \(f_x = 0\) when \(x=3\text{;}\) \(f_y = 0\) when \(y = 0\text{,}\) so one critical point at \((3,0)\text{,}\) which is a relative maximum, where \(f_{xx} = \frac{y^2-16}{(16-(x-3)^2-y^2)^{3/2}}\) and \(D = \frac{16}{(16-(x-3)^2-y^2)^{2}}\text{.}\)

Both \(f_x\) and \(f_y\) are undefined along the circle \((x-3)^2+y^2=16\text{;}\) at any point along this curve, \(f(x,y)=0\text{,}\) the absolute minimum of the function.
