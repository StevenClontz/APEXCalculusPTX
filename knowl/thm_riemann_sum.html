Theorem 5.3.26. Definite Integrals and the Limit of Riemann Sums.

Let \(f\) be continuous on the closed interval \([a,b]\) and let \(S_L(n)\text{,}\) \(S_R(n)\text{,}\) \(S_M(n)\text{,}\) \(\Delta x\text{,}\) \(\Delta x_i\) and \(c_i\) be defined as before. Then:

  1. \(\displaystyle \begin{aligned}\lim_{n\to\infty} S_L(n) \amp = \lim_{n\to\infty} S_R(n)\\ \amp = \lim_{n\to\infty} S_M(n)\\ \amp = \lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n f(c_i)\Delta x\end{aligned}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n f(c_i)\Delta x = \int_a^b f(x)\, dx\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{\norm{\Delta x}\to 0} \sum_{i=1}^n f(c_i)\Delta x_i = \int_a^b f(x)\, dx\)
