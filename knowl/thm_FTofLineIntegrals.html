Theorem 15.3.23. Fundamental Theorem of Line Integrals.

Let \(\vec F\) be a vector field whose components are continuous on a connected domain \(D\) in the plane or in space, let \(A\) and \(B\) be any points in \(D\text{,}\) and let \(C\) be any path in \(D\) starting at \(A\) and ending at \(B\text{.}\)

  1. \(\vec F\) is conservative if and only if there exists a differentiable function \(f\) such that \(\vec F = \nabla f\text{.}\)

  2. If \(\vec F\) is conservative, then

    \begin{equation*} \int_C\vec F\cdot d\vec r = \int_A^B \vec F\cdot d\vec r = f(B) - f(A)\text{.} \end{equation*}
