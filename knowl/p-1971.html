A property of logarithms is that \(\log_a(x) = \frac{\log_b(x)}{\log_b(a)}\text{,}\) for all bases \(a,b \gt 0,\neq 1\text{.}\)

  1. Rewrite this identity when \(b=e\text{,}\) i.e., using \(\log_e(x) =\ln(x)\text{,}\) with \(a=10\text{.}\)

  2. Use part (a) to find the derivative of \(y=\log_{10}(x)\text{.}\)

  3. Find the derivative of \(y=\log_a(x)\) for any \(a\gt0,\neq1\text{.}\)
