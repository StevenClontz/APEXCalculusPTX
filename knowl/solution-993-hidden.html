These are the sames lines as given in Example 11.5.9, where we showed them to be skew. The equations allow us to identify the following points and vectors:

\begin{equation*} P_1 = (1,2,0) P_2 = (-2,3,5) \Rightarrow \overrightarrow{P_1P_2} = \la -3,1,5\ra\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \vec d_1 = \la 3,-1,1\ra \vec d_2 = \la 4,1,2\ra \Rightarrow \vec c = \vec d_1\times \vec d_2 = \la -3,-2,7\ra\text{.} \end{equation*}

From Key Idea 11.5.17 we have the distance \(h\) between the two lines is

\begin{align*} h \amp = \frac{\abs{\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\cdot \vec c}}{\vnorm c}\\ \amp =\frac{42}{\sqrt{62}} \approx 5.334\text{.} \end{align*}

The lines are approximately 5.334 units apart.
