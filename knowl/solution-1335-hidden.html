The triangle is bound by the lines \(y=-1\text{,}\) \(y=2x+1\) and \(y=-2x+1\text{.}\)

Along \(y=-1\text{,}\) there is a critical point at \((0,-1)\text{.}\)

Along \(y=2x+1\text{,}\) there is a critical point at \((-3/5,-1/5)\text{.}\)

Along \(y=-2x+1\text{,}\) there is a critical point at \((3/5,-1/5)\text{.}\)

The function \(f\) has one critical point, irrespective of the constraint, at \((0,-1/2)\text{.}\)

Checking the value of \(f\) at these four points, along with the three vertices of the triangle, we find the absolute maximum is at \((0,1,3)\) and the absolute minimum is at \((0,-1/2,3/4)\text{.}\)
