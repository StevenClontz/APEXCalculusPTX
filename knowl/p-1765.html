Recall that the tangent line to \(f\) at \(x=c\) is \(y = \fp(c)(x-c)+f(c)\text{.}\) While \(f\) is not explicitly given, by the graph it looks like \(f(3) = 4\text{.}\) Recalling that \(\fp(3) = 3\text{,}\) we can compute the tangent line to be approximately \(y = 3(x-3)+4\text{.}\) It is often useful to leave the tangent line in point-slope form.
