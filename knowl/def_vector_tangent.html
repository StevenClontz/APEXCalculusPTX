Definition 12.2.17. Tangent Vector, Tangent Line.

Let \(\vec r(t)\) be a differentiable vector-valued function on an open interval \(I\) containing \(c\text{,}\) where \(\vrp(c)\neq \vec 0\text{.}\)

  1. A vector \(\vec v\) is tangent to the graph of \(\vec r(t)\) at \(t=c\) if \(\vec v\) is parallel to \(\vrp(c)\text{.}\)

  2. The tangent line to the graph of \(\vec r(t)\) at \(t=c\) is the line through \(\vec r(c)\) with direction parallel to \(\vrp(c)\text{.}\) An equation of the tangent line is

    \begin{equation*} \vec \ell(t) = \vec r(c) + t\,\vrp(c)\text{.} \end{equation*}
