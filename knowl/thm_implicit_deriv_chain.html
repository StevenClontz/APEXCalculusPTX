Theorem 13.5.12. Implicit Differentiation.

Let \(f\) be a differentiable function of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) where \(f(x,y)=c\) defines \(y\) as an implicit function of \(x\text{,}\) for some constant \(c\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = - \frac{f_x(x,y)}{f_y(x,y)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
