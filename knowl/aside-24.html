Practicality.

Example 4.2.7 is both interesting and impractical. It highlights the difficulty in using radar in a nonlinear fashion, and explains why “in real life” the police officer would follow the other driver to determine their speed, and not pull out pencil and paper.

The principles here are important, though. Many automated vehicles make judgments about other moving objects based on perceived distances, radar-like measurements and the concepts of related rates.
