Find the equation of the tangent line to the graph of the implicitly defined function at the indicated points. As a visual aid, the function is graphed.

27.

On the curve \(x^{2/5}+y^{2/5} = 1\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle (1,0)\)

  2. \((0.1,0.2811)\) (which does not exactly lie on the curve, but is very close)

28.

On the curve \(x^{4}+y^{4} = 1\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle (1,0)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(\sqrt{0.6},\sqrt{0.8}\right)\)

  3. At \((0,1)\)

29.

On the curve \((x^2+y^2-4)^3 = 108y^2\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle (0,4)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(2,-\sqrt[4]{108}\right)\)

30.

On the curve \((x^2+y^2+x)^2 = x^2+y^2\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle (0,1)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(-\frac{3}{4}, \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{4}\right)\)

31.

On the curve \((x-2)^2+(y-3)^2=9\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{7}{2},\frac{6+3\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{4+3\sqrt{3}}{2},\frac{3}{2}\right)\)

32.

On the curve \(x^2+y^3+2xy=0\text{,}\) find the equation of the tangent line at:

  1. \(\displaystyle (-1,1)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(-1,\frac12(-1+\sqrt{5})\right)\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \left(-1,\frac12(-1-\sqrt{5})\right)\)
