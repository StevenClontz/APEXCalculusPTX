For any \(x\text{,}\) \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} \frac{x^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} = 0\text{.}\) Applying the Squeeze Theorem to Equation (9.8.1), we conclude that \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} R_n(x) = 0\) for all \(x\text{,}\) and hence

\begin{equation*} \cos(x) = \infser[0] (-1)^{n}\frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!} \text{ for all \(x\) }\text{.} \end{equation*}
