In other words, the \(x\) and \(y\) in \(g(x,y)\) are not the same as the ones in \(f(x,y,z)\text{!}\) If this is still confusing, try setting \(x=s\text{,}\) \(y=t\text{,}\) and \(z=g(s,t)\text{,}\) and then applying the chain rule as usual, for derivatives of \(f(x,y,z)\) with respect to \(s\) and \(t\text{.}\) At the end, we can relabel \(s\) and \(t\) as \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)
