As \(\vec r(t)\) has constant length, \(\vec r(t)\cdot\vec r(t)=c^2\) for some constant \(c\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{align*} \vec r(t)\cdot\vec r(t) \amp = c^2\\ \frac{d}{dt}\big(\vec r(t)\cdot\vec r(t)\big) \amp = \frac{d}{dt}\big(c^2\big)\\ \vrp(t)\cdot \vec r(t)+\vec r(t)\cdot \vrp(t) \amp = 0\\ 2\vec r(t)\cdot \vrp(t) \amp =0\\ \vec r(t)\cdot \vrp(t) \amp =0\text{.} \end{align*}
