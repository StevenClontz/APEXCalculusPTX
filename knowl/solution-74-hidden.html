We proceed by examining the three criteria for continuity.

  1. The limits \(\lim\limits_{x\to c} f(x)\) exists for all \(c\) between \(0\) and \(3\text{.}\)

  2. \(f(c)\) is defined for all \(c\) between \(0\) and \(3\text{,}\) except for \(c=1\text{.}\) We know immediately that \(f\) cannot be continuous at \(x=1\text{.}\)

  3. The limit \(\lim\limits_{x\to c} f(x) = f(c)\) for all \(c\) between \(0\) and \(3\text{,}\) except, of course, for \(c=1\text{.}\)

We conclude that \(f\) is continuous at every point of the interval \((0,3)\) except at \(x=1\text{.}\) Therefore \(f\) is continuous on \((0,1)\) and \((1,3)\text{.}\)
