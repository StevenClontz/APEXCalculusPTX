Solution 9.8.16.1.

Since \(f(x)\) is a composition, our first instict might be to apply TheoremÂ 9.8.12 to the problem. However, \(\sqrt{x}\) is not a polynomial function, and neither \(\ln(x)\) nor \(\sqrt{x}\) have Maclaurin series expansions.

You might already see a simple way to proceed, but let us first consider the following: \(\sqrt{x}=(1+(x-1))^{1/2}\) can be expanded as a binomial series centered at \(x=1\text{.}\) We also know the Taylor series for \(\ln(x)\) at \(x=1\text{,}\) and note that \(\sqrt{1}=1\text{,}\) so when \(x\) is near 1, so is \(\sqrt{x}\text{.}\)

What happens if we take the Taylor series

\begin{equation*} \ln(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1}\frac{(x-1)^n}{n} = (x-1)-\frac{(x-1)^2}{2}+\frac{(x-1)^3}{3}-\cdots \end{equation*}

and substitute in

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{x} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \binom{1/2}{n}(x-1)^n = 1+\frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-1)^2+\cdots\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\binom{1/2}{n}=\frac{1/2(1/2-1)\cdots (1/2-(n-1))}{n!}\) denotes the binomial coefficient?

Short answer: a mess. We have to replace each occurence of \(x-1\) in the power series for \(\ln(x)\) with \(\sqrt{x}-1 = \frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{16}(x-1)^3+\cdots\text{,}\) and then expand, and collect terms. If we do this, keeping only terms up to \((x-1)^3\text{,}\) we find:

\begin{align*} \ln(\sqrt{x}) \amp = \left(\frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{16}(x-1)^3+\cdots\right)\\ \amp \quad -\frac12\left(\frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{16}(x-1)^3+\cdots\right)^2 \\ \amp \quad + \frac13\left(\frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{16}(x-1)^3+\cdots\right)+\cdots\\ \amp = \frac12(x-1)-\frac14(x-2)^2+\frac16(x-1)^3-\cdots\text{.} \end{align*}

But of course, there was a better way all along:

\begin{equation*} \ln(\sqrt{x}) = \ln(x^{1/2}) = \frac12\ln(x) \end{equation*}

using properties of the logarithm, and indeed, the result above is the same as the one we would have obtained by simply multiplying the Taylor series for \(\ln(x)\) by \(\frac12\text{.}\) Power series manipulation is a powerful technique, but one should not apply it blindly.
