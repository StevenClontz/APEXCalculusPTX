Solution 9.8.20.1.

Given \(\ds \cos(x) = \infser[0] (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!}\text{,}\)

\(\ds\frac{d}{dx}\big(\cos(x) \big) = \frac{d}{dx}\left(\infser[0] (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!}\right)=\infser (-1)^n\frac{(2n)x^{2n-1}}{(2n)!}=\infser (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n-1}}{(2n-1)!}\text{.}\) We can re-index this summation to start at \(n=0\) by replacing \(n\) with \(n+1\) in the summation:

\begin{equation*} \infser (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n-1}}{(2n-1)!} =\infser[0] (-1)^{n+1}\frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Note that this series has the opposite sign of the Taylor series for \(\sin(x)\text{;}\) thus \(\frac{d}{dx}(\cos(x) ) = -\sin(x)\text{.}\)
