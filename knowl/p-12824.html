We begin by setting \(f(x,y)=c\) for an arbitrary \(c\) and seeing if algebraic manipulation of the equation reveals anything significant.

\begin{align*} \frac{x+y}{x^2+y^2+1} \amp = c\\ x+y \amp = c(x^2+y^2+1).\\ \end{align*}

We recognize this as a circle, though the center and radius are not yet clear. By completing the square, we can obtain:

\begin{align*} \left(x-\frac{1}{2c}\right)^2+\left(y-\frac1{2c}\right)^2\amp =\frac{1}{2c^2}-1\text{,} \end{align*}

a circle centered at \(\big(1/(2c),1/(2c)\big)\) with radius \(\sqrt{1/(2c^2)-1}\text{,}\) where \(\abs{c}\lt 1/\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) The level curves for \(c=\pm 0.2,\,\pm 0.4\) and \(\pm0.6\) are sketched in Figure 13.1.11.(a). To help illustrate “elevation,” we use thicker lines for \(c\) values near 0, and dashed lines indicate where \(c\lt 0\text{.}\)
