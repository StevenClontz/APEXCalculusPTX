Key Idea 10.4.6. Converting Between Rectangular and Polar Coordinates.

Given the polar point \(P(r,\theta)\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates are determined by

\begin{equation*} x=r\cos(\theta) \qquad y=r\sin(\theta)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Given the rectangular coordinates \((x,y)\text{,}\) the polar coordinates are determined by

\begin{equation*} r^2=x^2+y^2\qquad \tan(\theta) = \frac yx\text{.} \end{equation*}
