  1. Let \(f\) be a function whose \((n+1)\text{th }\) derivative exists on an interval \(I\) and let \(c\) be in \(I\text{.}\) Then, for each \(x\) in \(I\text{,}\) there exists \(z_x\) between \(x\) and \(c\) such that

    \begin{equation*} \hspace{-25pt}f(x) = f(c) + \fp(c)(x-c) + \frac{\fpp(c)}{2!}(x-c)^2+ \cdots +\frac{f^{(n)}(c)}{n!}(x-c)^n+R_n(x)\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(\ds R_n(x) = \frac{f^{(n+1)}(z_x)}{(n+1)!}(x-c)^{(n+1)}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\ds \abs{R_n(x)} \leq \frac{\max\abs{\,f^{(n+1)}(z)}}{(n+1)!}\abs{(x-c)^{(n+1)}}\text{,}\) where \(z\) is in \(I\text{.}\)
