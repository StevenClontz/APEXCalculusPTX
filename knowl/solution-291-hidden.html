To compute the areas of the 5 trapezoids in Figure 5.5.7, it will again be useful to create a table of values as shown in Figure 5.5.10.

The leftmost trapezoid has legs of length \(1\) and \(0.9607\) and a height of 0.2. Thus, by our formula, the area of the leftmost trapezoid is:

\begin{equation*} \frac{1+0.9608}{2}(0.2) = 0.1961\text{.} \end{equation*}

Moving right, the next trapezoid has legs of length \(0.9607\) and \(0.8521\) and a height of 0.2. Thus its area is:

\begin{equation*} \frac{0.9608+0.8521}2(0.2) = 0.1813\text{.} \end{equation*}

The sum of the areas of all 5 trapezoids is:

\begin{align*} \frac{1+0.9608}{2}(0.2) + \frac{0.9608+0.8521}2(0.2)+\frac{0.8521+0.6977}2(0.2)\amp +\\ \frac{0.6977+0.5273}2(0.2)+\frac{0.5273+0.3679}2(0.2)\amp = 0.7444\text{.} \end{align*}

We approximate \(\int_0^1 e^{-x^2}\, dx \approx 0.7444\text{.}\)
