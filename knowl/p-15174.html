Since density is constant, we set \(\delta = 1\) and finding the mass is equivalent to finding the volume of the solid. We set up the triple integral to compute this but do not evaluate it; we leave it to the reader to confirm it evaluates to the same result found in ExampleÂ 14.3.13.

\begin{equation*} M = \iiint_D\delta \, dV = \int_0^{\pi}\int_0^{\cos(3\theta)}\int_0^{1-r\cos(\theta)+0.1r\sin(\theta)} r\,dz\,dr\,d\theta \approx 0.785\text{.} \end{equation*}
