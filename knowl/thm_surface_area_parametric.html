Theorem 10.3.21. Surface Area of a Solid of Revolution.

Consider the graph of the parametric equations \(x=f(t)\) and \(y=g(t)\text{,}\) where \(\fp\) and \(g'\) are continuous on an open interval \(I\) containing \(t_1\) and \(t_2\) on which the graph does not cross itself.

  1. The surface area of the solid formed by revolving the graph about the \(x\)-axis is (where \(g(t)\geq 0\) on \([t_1,t_2]\)):

    \begin{equation*} \text{ Surface Area } = 2\pi\int_{t_1}^{t_2} g(t)\sqrt{\fp(t)^2+g'(t)^2}\, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. The surface area of the solid formed by revolving the graph about the \(y\)-axis is (where \(f(t)\geq 0\) on \([t_1,t_2]\)):

    \begin{equation*} \text{ Surface Area } = 2\pi\int_{t_1}^{t_2} f(t)\sqrt{\fp(t)^2+g'(t)^2}\, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
