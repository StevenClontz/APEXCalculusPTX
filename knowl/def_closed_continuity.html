Definition 1.5.7. Continuity on Closed Intervals.

Let \(f\) be defined on the closed interval \([a,b]\) for some real numbers \(a\lt b\text{.}\) We say \(f\) is continuous on the closed interval \([a,b]\) if:

  1. \(f\) is continuous on \((a,b)\text{,}\)

  2. \(\lim\limits_{x\to a^+} f(x) = f(a)\) and

  3. \(\lim\limits_{x\to b^-} f(x) = f(b)\text{.}\)
