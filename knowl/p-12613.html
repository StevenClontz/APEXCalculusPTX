Solving for \(\vrp(s)\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation} \vrp(s) = \frac{\vrp(t)}{\norm{\vrp(t)}} = \unittangent(t)\text{,}\tag{12.5.3} \end{equation}

where \(\unittangent(t)\) is the unit tangent vector. EquationÂ (12.5.3) is often misinterpreted, as one is tempted to think it states \(\vrp(t) = \unittangent(t)\text{,}\) but there is a big difference between \(\vrp(s)\) and \(\vrp(t)\text{.}\) The key to take from it is that \(\vrp(s)\) is a unit vector.
