In cylindrical coordinates, the density is \(\delta(r,\theta,z) = r+1\text{.}\) Thus mass is

\begin{equation*} M=\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^2\int_0^4 (r+1)r\, dz\, dr\, d\theta = 112\pi/3\text{.} \end{equation*}

We find \(M_{yz} = 0\text{,}\) \(M_{xz} = 0\text{,}\) and \(M_{xy} = 224\pi/3\text{,}\) placing the center of mass at \((0,0,2)\text{.}\)
