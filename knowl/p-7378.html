The top region lies in the \(y\)-interval of \([3,6]\text{,}\) where the length of the differential element is 25 ft as shown. As the pool is 20 ft wide, this differential element represents a thin slice of water with volume \(V(y) = 20\cdot25\cdot dy\text{.}\) The water is to be pumped to a height of \(y=8\text{,}\) so the height function is \(h(y) = 8-y\text{.}\) The work done in pumping this top region of water is

\begin{equation*} W_t = 62.4\int_3^6 500(8-y)\, dy = 327,600 \,\text{ft--lb}\text{.} \end{equation*}
