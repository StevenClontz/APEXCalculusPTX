Solution 14.7.3.42.1.

Rectangular: \(\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\, dz\, dy\, dx\)

Cylindrical: \(\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{\sec(\theta)}\int_{0}^{1}r\, dz\, dr\, d\theta + \int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{\csc(\theta)}\int_{0}^{1}r\, dz\, dr\, d\theta\)

Spherical:

\begin{align*} \amp \int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{\tan^{-1}(\sec(\theta))}\int_0^{\csc(\varphi)}\rho^2 \cos(\varphi)\, d\rho\, d\varphi\, d\theta\\ \amp\quad+\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_{\tan^{-1}(\sec(\theta))}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{\sec(\theta)\sec(\varphi)}\rho^2 \cos(\varphi)\, d\rho\, d\varphi\, d\theta\\ \amp\quad+\int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{\tan^{-1}(\csc(\theta))}\int_0^{\csc(\varphi)}\rho^2 \cos(\varphi)\, d\rho\, d\varphi\, d\theta\\ \amp\quad+\int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}\int_{\tan^{-1}(\csc(\theta))}^{\pi/2}\int_0^{\csc(\theta)\sec(\varphi)}\rho^2 \cos(\varphi)\, d\rho\, d\varphi\, d\theta\text{.} \end{align*}

Rectangular is clearly the simplest.
